## 6. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

- **Is Jiu Jitsu only for young people?**
  "No. Jiu Jitsu is for all people, all shapes, all sizes and all AGES. Helio Gracie trained until he was 95!"
- **Is it hard?**
  "It is not easy to master, but you will see results from early on and be able to participate from day 1."
- **Is it MMA?**
  "No. While it's a critical skill for MMA, BJJ has no kicks or punches. It revolves around leverage and manipulation."
- **Is it dangerous?**
  "Like any contact sport it comes with risks, but BJJ is one of the safest martial arts because it minimizes striking and focuses on technique."

## 7. Rules & Code of Conduct

### Fee Policy
1. Contract is month-to-month.
2. One month's notice required for termination.
3. Fees payable in advance by the 3rd of each month.
4. No payment holidays for standard holidays or absences.

### Safety & Liability
- Respect the "Kinetic Precision" of the mats.
- Remove all jewellery before training.
- "Everyone is family so be careful – we need each other to train, so do not break your toys, you need them tomorrow."
- Alert instructors of any injuries or medical conditions before class.

### Hygiene (The "Gunk Master" Rule)
- You must wash your Gi, rashies, and equipment after **every** session.
- Keep fingernails and toenails clipped ("scratching is not a technique").
- Do not come to the gym with skin diseases (ringworm, staph, etc.).

### Etiquette & Hierarchy
- **Respect the hierarchy:** Lower belts move for higher belts when space is tight on the mats. Higher-ranking teammates and instructors always have right-of-way.
- **Rolling/Sparring:** The aim is to practice, not to beat or hurt each other. "The junior partner sets the pace."
- Be gracious in victory and defeat. Do not brag about submissions.
